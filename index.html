Iterative Design Process

Understanding design evolution is critical to becoming an exemplary designer. Below is my approach to iterative refinement:

1. Ideation & Brainstorming

I begin each project by exploring multiple directions. For game projects, I sketch mechanics, consider player emotions, and identify potential challenges early. Brainstorming documentation captures raw ideas and conceptual exploration.

2. Wireframes & Sketches

Initial wireframes and flowcharts visualize system architecture and user flow. For games, this includes control schemes, UI layouts, and level progression. Visual planning prevents misalignment between vision and implementation.

3. Prototype & Playtest

I create functional prototypes to test core mechanics. Playtesting reveals what works and what needs refinement. User feedback is invaluable—what I assumed would be engaging often surprises me.

4. Refinement & Polish

Based on feedback, I iterate on mechanics, pacing, and presentation. Refinements address both technical issues and design problems. This cycle repeats until reaching the desired quality threshold.
